Curriculum Overview

A thorough explanation of Washington University Nursery School’s philosophy and educational practices may be found in the Curriculum Overview

Course name Course Description
Teddy Bears The Teddy Bear class is the morning session for 2 1/2 – and 3-year-old children. Teachers design a variety of engaging activities that gradually encourage children to interact and communicate with others. The indoor classroom and outdoor playground are safe, interesting spaces for Teddy Bears to explore and make sense of their world.
Panda Bears The Panda Bear class is the afternoon session for 2 1/2 – and 3-year-old children. Teachers design a variety of engaging activities that gradually encourage children to interact and communicate with others. The indoor classroom and outdoor playground are safe, interesting spaces for Panda Bears to explore and make sense of their world.
Bear Cubs The Bear Cub class is the morning session for 3- and 4-year-old children. Their growing communication and motor skills allow these preschoolers to become more self-sufficient. Teacher guidance and routines in the classroom help build self-esteem and confidence in the children as they enjoy individual, small-group and large-group activities.
Big Bears The Big Bear class is the morning session for 4- and 5-year old children. Classroom activities build on previous learning experiences, and children’s interests help drive projects and collaborative play. Teachers support each child’s readiness for kindergarten across all academic, social and emotional areas.
Sun Bears The Sun Bear class is the afternoon session for 4- and 5-year old children. Classroom activities build on previous learning experiences, and children’s interests help drive projects and collaborative play. Teachers support each child’s readiness for kindergarten across all academic, social and emotional areas.
Bear Tracks The Bear Cub class is the morning session for 3- and 4-year-old children. Their growing communication and motor skills allow these preschoolers to become more self-sufficient. Teacher guidance and routines in the classroom help build self-esteem and confidence in the children as they enjoy individual, small-group and large-group activities.
Enrichment Programs In addition to their classroom teachers, all children enrolled in the Washington University Nursery School interact regularly with specialists in art, music, Spanish and science.
